UI/UX Case Study

Applebttm

DES 112 Gabriel Figueroa

Applebttm is a music review and discovery platform inspired by Goodreads and Letterboxd, designed to let users share opinions, rate albums, and uncover new artists. Built around intuitive navigation and streamlined onboarding, the app balances social engagement with simplicity to reduce cognitive overload.

Overview

Applebttm is a music review and discovery platform designed to fill a gap in the digital landscape: while Goodreads (books) and Letterboxd (films) exist, there has been no equivalent for music. Applebttm enables users to rate albums, write reviews, make lists, and discover new artists through a socially engaging, intuitive interface. This project was developed as a project for DES 112 at UC Davis, where I led UX research, prototyping, and usability testing.

The Problem

Despite music being a central part of culture, there is no dedicated platform where users can review albums and share opinions in the same way readers and film fans do. Existing review apps for other media often overwhelm users with cluttered feeds, frontloaded onboarding, or poorly designed activity tabs. The challenge was to create a platform that preserves the social joy of discovery while reducing cognitive overload.

Research & Insights

I began by analyzing the strengths and weaknesses of apps like Goodreads and Letterboxd. Focus groups revealed that users appreciated the social aspects (ranking, reviews, activity feeds), but disliked being shown every action from friends and wanted smarter ways to discover new content.

Key insights

Strengths

Social ranking, list-making, community interaction.

Weakness

Overload of information, confusing navigation, limited personalization.

Opportunities

Spotlight rising artists, enable curated feeds, design for shareable moments.

Threats

Risk of being too similar to competitors or devolving into spam.

These insights shaped the foundation of Applebttm.

Design Goals

  • Reduce onboarding friction and cognitive overload.
  • Provide intuitive navigation through clear iconography and simple flows.
  • Spotlight new and upcoming artists to encourage music discovery.

The Solution

The final prototype is structured around four core tabs:

  1. Profile: Users see all their reviews chronologically and access their lists.
  2. Create: A simple flow for rating albums and posting reviews.
  3. Activity: A curated feed of friends’ activity designed to reduce clutter.
  4. Discover: A hub highlighting trending albums, top reviews, and up-and-coming artists.

These design choices emphasized clarity and usability while keeping the social discovery aspect fun.

Usability Testing

I conducted usability tests with five participants including a mix of peers and non-designers. Key findings included:

Onboarding

Users wanted more control → added back/skip options.

Activity Feed

Lightning-bolt nav bar icon was unclear and the feed was overwhelming → replaced icon, simplified layout, increased text legibility.

Rating Albums

Users weren’t sure how to start reviews → clarified icons, added text input, improved flow.

These refinements made the app more approachable and user-friendly.

Impact & Takeaways

Applebttm reinforced the importance of iterative testing—what seems obvious to a designer may be confusing to users. I learned how critical it is to balance engagement with clarity, especially in socially driven platforms. The project also gave me hands-on experience with prototyping in Figma and running usability tests that directly informed design decisions.
